>Studio thinking framework:
>
>Why do we teach art in schools?
>
>- falsely claimed that arts students did better in testing, correlation but not causation
>- arts teaches specific set of skills that's not present elsewhere
>- standardized tests narrow the scope of what schools are incentivized to teach
>- testing rewards memorization, not depth or desire to learn which are considered better predictors of success
>- some arts habits are: visual-spatial abilities, reflection, self-criticism, willingness to experiment, learn from mistakes.
>
>Author found the following habits interesting
>- observing, envisioning, innnovating through exploration, and reflective self-evaluation = these are all hard to assess but valuable in life
>- seeing is framed by expectation, expectation gets in the way of perception (ie. drawing a face, we usually draw the eyes closer to the forehead, hands smaller than our face) -- observational drawing requires seeing accurately
>* from personal studies, sometimes you are encouraged to look at negative spaces or to generalize spaces into easier to draw shapes
>- seeing past preconceptions is handy for other professions to accurately diagnose or classify
>- envisioning: what would it look like if you did [x] (common in cs interviews)
>* aphantasia and pixar
>- innovation: we can encourage students to take risks by adding or subtracting friction or incentives where possible
>- Boston Arts Academy "i want you to play around in this first project... see what happens"
>* in doing this, they may discover techniques that the teacher can reinforce by sharing the name of the technique for convergent discovery
>- let mistakes lead to discoveries
>- constant self-reflection / evaluation
>- "is that working, what i intended to do? make this better?
>- teachers talked more about decisions, choices, and understanding more than feelings.
>- arts can be used as a tool to balance what is currently skewed towards readily testable skills and information
>* how can we get students to learn to embrace the messiness of iteration, and stray further from perfectionism
>- ron berger 5th grade teacher in mass: creates long-term investigations over one-shot assignments
