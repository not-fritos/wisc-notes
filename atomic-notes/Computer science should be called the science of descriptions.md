> > Computers are called upon to do many things, and getting a computer to do something requires that the underlying process be described, o~ some level, with enough precision to be carried out by the machine. Thus computer scientists have devoted much of their talent and energy to developing powerful descriptive formalisms. One might even say that computer science is wrongly so called: Most of it is not the science of computers, but the science of descriptions and descriptive languages
