> > The design of ill- and well-structured problem scenarios was closely aligned to Voss’ (1988, 2005) and Jonassen’s (2000) design typologies for problems. Accordingly, ill-structured problems were designed such that they: 
> > - possessed many problem parameters with some unknown, or known only with some degree of confidence, 
> > - possessed problem parameters that interacted with each other in interesting ways such that the effect of each could not be examined in isolation, 
> > - possessed multiple solutions and solution paths, 
> > - possessed multiple criteria for evaluating solutions, and 
> > - required learners to make assumptions, judgments, and express personal opinions or beliefs.
