> > Content analysis suggested that three critical elements—problem representation, nature of assumptions, and contextual factors—distinguished the solutions produced by students from IS groups from those produced by students from WS groups. In other words, the frequency of occurrence of these elements in students’ solutions and the manner in which they were combined to develop quantitative and qualitative arguments (which the rubric in Table 4 rated as part of its holistic rating of solution quality) seemed to explain, in part, the better performance of students from IS groups on the well-structured problems post-test.
