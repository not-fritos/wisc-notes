> > This article uses an embedded multiple-case methodology (Yin, 2013) to explore (a) the ways in which the historical narrative regarding STEM and STEM pathways was taken up in local discourse and practices at the young women’s high schools; (b) the kinds of identities these women constructed over the course of high school and into college; and (c) how these young women instantiated and negotiated STEM identities in the context of their schools.
