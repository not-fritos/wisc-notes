> > This approach of intentionally placing students in courses seemed to be working. According to the principal, the number of students in AP courses had increased, and, as stated earlier by the principal, students were seeing grade-level gains of up to 4 years. This at times required students to “double up” on math courses in order to take AP Calculus by their senior year. In addition, students at grade level being pushed to get on the “honors track” were required to take advanced coursework, including honors, AP, and college-level courses (offered through a partnership with a local community college).
